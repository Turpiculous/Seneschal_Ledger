A Necessary Precaution

REGARDING UNAUTHORISED PERUSAL

To the Esteemed Initiate: You will have noted, undoubtedly, that the volume you currently hold is bound in a rather unremarkable, slightly scuffed calfskin, bearing an embossed title regarding the storage of winter linens. This is by meticulous design. True security lies in utter, unremitting tedium. No self-respecting thief of the Free City has ever stolen a laundry ledger.

However, should a particularly desperate footpad, or worse, an inquisitive employer, breach the clasp, they will immediately encounter the secondary measure. The vellum pages of this treatise have been delicately dusted with a proprietary compound derived from the Potter's Lead Glaze and Nightshade.

To the Unauthorised Reader: If you are reading these words, and you are not an active, dues-paying member of the League of the Silver Key, I am afraid you have already absorbed a fatal dose of the aforementioned compound through the pores of your fingers. You have approximately four minutes before the paralysis reaches your diaphragm. I strongly suggest you use this time to find a comfortable, un-upholstered surface upon which to expire. Bodily fluids are frightfully difficult to lift from velvet, and we really must insist upon maintaining a tidy estate.

Yours in tidy finality,
P. Sterling, Guildmaster

Introduction to the League

To the uninitiated observer traversing the pristine avenues of Verbobonc, the Academy of Domestic Excellence is merely the premier finishing school for the Flanaess's elite domestic staff. To those within our fraternity, however, it is the public guise of the League of the Silver Key. We are an esteemed brotherhood of domestic perfectionists, dedicated equally to maintaining the immaculate tailoring of our employers and ensuring their continued respiration.

A true agent of the League seamlessly manages his master's social calendar, polishes his boots to a mirror sheen, and quietly disposes of enemy cutthroats without ever spilling a single drop of blood upon the Baklunish carpets. We do not murder for coin; we proactively dismantle threats to maintain the pristine order of our appointed households.

The Haberdasher's Cipher

Should you encounter a fellow seneschal whilst in the employ of a different House, it is vital to communicate the status of your wardrobe without arousing suspicion. Memorise the following phrases:

  • "The winter woollens require an airing." A threat to the Master has been identified and must be disposed of outside the bounds of the estate.
  • "I fear the claret has stained the damask." An intruder has been neutralised indoors; require immediate assistance with sanitisation and alibis.
  • "The Master requires a stiffer collar." The current employer is weak-willed and susceptible to magical charm or coercion. Heightened defensive posture required.
  • "The moths have found the cashmere." The estate is compromised by an unseen rogue or spy. Initiate lockdown protocols and prepare the hidden armaments.

The Art of the Mundane Arsenal

It is a truth universally acknowledged within our profession that the modern gentleman is frequently prone to attracting the attentions of individuals whose motives are entirely disagreeable. We at the League operate under the firm conviction that overt violence is fundamentally vulgar.

Consequently, it falls upon the gentleman's personal gentleman to manage these unseemly disruptions. The amateur assassin reaches for a conspicuous poisoned dagger. The professional counter-assassin recognises that the average estate is positively brimming with objects of profound lethality and utility, provided one possesses the imagination to apply them correctly.

6. Requisitions From the Marketplace

"The market is not merely for acquiring truffles and fine wine; it is an auxiliary armoury. Remember poor Jenkins, who forgot his sack of dried lentils and was forced to engage a ruffian using only a rather firm rutabaga. He survived, but the indignity has forever stained his references."
01 - High-Proof Brandy (1 lb)
Concealability: Pocket
Warms cold carriage rides. Sterilises wounds effectively, or functions as a devastating incendiary device.
Ignites instantly when exposed to a spark. Poured under a door, it creates a wall of fire dealing 1d6 damage to anyone attempting a breach.
02 - Block of Animal Fat (1 lb)
Concealability: Pocket
Culinary preservation. Rubbed generously on squeaky armour joints or rusted hinges to ensure utter silence.
Reduces the stealth penalty for wearing heavy armour by 10%. Greasing a sloped floor creates a hazard requiring a Dexterity check.

Concealability: Satchel
Hangs heavy carcasses. Tied to a stout rope to act as a robust grappling hook for scaling manor walls.

04 - Dried Chestnuts (1 lb)
Concealability: Pocket
A healthy snack. Scattered heavily in dark corridors to crunch loudly under errant boots.
Acts as a perfectly mundane alarm system. Impossible to step on silently, negating an approaching enemy's Move Silently skill.

Concealability: Pocket
Used in parlour games. Rolled silently onto the floor to test for subtle, trap-inducing slopes.

06 - Strong Fishing Gut (Negligible)
Concealability: Pocket
For weekend angling. Nearly invisible in the dark for discreet tripwires or safely retrieving objects.

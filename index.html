A Necessary Precaution

REGARDING UNAUTHORISED PERUSAL

To the Esteemed Initiate: You will have noted, undoubtedly, that the volume you currently hold is bound in a rather unremarkable, slightly scuffed calfskin, bearing an embossed title regarding the storage of winter linens. This is by meticulous design. True security lies in utter, unremitting tedium. No self-respecting thief of the Free City has ever stolen a laundry ledger.

However, should a particularly desperate footpad, or worse, an inquisitive employer, breach the clasp, they will immediately encounter the secondary measure. The vellum pages of this treatise have been delicately dusted with a proprietary compound derived from the Potter's Lead Glaze and Nightshade.

To the Unauthorised Reader: If you are reading these words, and you are not an active, dues-paying member of the League of the Silver Key, I am afraid you have already absorbed a fatal dose of the aforementioned compound through the pores of your fingers. You have approximately four minutes before the paralysis reaches your diaphragm. I strongly suggest you use this time to find a comfortable, un-upholstered surface upon which to expire. Bodily fluids are frightfully difficult to lift from velvet, and we really must insist upon maintaining a tidy estate.

Yours in tidy finality,
P. Sterling, Guildmaster

Introduction to the League

To the uninitiated observer traversing the pristine avenues of Verbobonc, the Academy of Domestic Excellence is merely the premier finishing school for the Flanaess's elite domestic staff. To those within our fraternity, however, it is the public guise of the League of the Silver Key. We are an esteemed brotherhood of domestic perfectionists, dedicated equally to maintaining the immaculate tailoring of our employers and ensuring their continued respiration.

A true agent of the League seamlessly manages his master's social calendar, polishes his boots to a mirror sheen, and quietly disposes of enemy cutthroats without ever spilling a single drop of blood upon the Baklunish carpets. We do not murder for coin; we proactively dismantle threats to maintain the pristine order of our appointed households.

The Haberdasher's Cipher

Should you encounter a fellow seneschal whilst in the employ of a different House, it is vital to communicate the status of your wardrobe without arousing suspicion. Memorise the following phrases:

  • "The winter woollens require an airing." A threat to the Master has been identified and must be disposed of outside the bounds of the estate.
  • "I fear the claret has stained the damask." An intruder has been neutralised indoors; require immediate assistance with sanitisation and alibis.
  • "The Master requires a stiffer collar." The current employer is weak-willed and susceptible to magical charm or coercion. Heightened defensive posture required.
  • "The moths have found the cashmere." The estate is compromised by an unseen rogue or spy. Initiate lockdown protocols and prepare the hidden armaments.

The Art of the Mundane Arsenal

It is a truth universally acknowledged within our profession that the modern gentleman is frequently prone to attracting the attentions of individuals whose motives are entirely disagreeable. We at the League operate under the firm conviction that overt violence is fundamentally vulgar.

Consequently, it falls upon the gentleman's personal gentleman to manage these unseemly disruptions. The amateur assassin reaches for a conspicuous poisoned dagger. The professional counter-assassin recognises that the average estate is positively brimming with objects of profound lethality and utility, provided one possesses the imagination to apply them correctly.

APPENDIX I: THE SENESCHAL'S PROBLEM-SOLVING INDEX

A Quick-Reference Guide for Sudden Indiscretions

"A gentleman’s gentleman does not panic when confronted by a gelatinous cube, a jammed portcullis, or an assassination attempt during the salad course. He simply diagnoses the inconvenience, selects the appropriate implement, and restores order before the Master's tea grows cold." - P. Sterling

Field Agent Note: Memorise these categorisations. When the dungeon collapses, you will not have time to read alphabetically. - Initiate Thorne

I. Apprehending & Silencing the Uncouth

When a sentry must be quietly removed from his post without the vulgarity of drawn blood.

  • The Silent Chokehold: Silk Handkerchiefs [1.05], Cheese Wire [2.01], Measuring Tape [8.11]
    Resolves as Grapple; silences verbal spellcasting.
  • The Polite Concussion: Hardwood Rolling Pin [2.16], Brass Paperweight [3.06], Carpenter's Mallet [8.03], White Formal Gloves [10.07]
    x2 Backstab multiplier for knockout blows.
  • Chemical Slumber: Laudanum Tincture [4.10], Church Incense Cones [10.19]
    Save vs. Poison or fall into deep sleep.

II. Misdirection & Sensory Deprivation

When the party must pass unseen, or an enemy spellcaster must be violently interrupted mid-incantation.

  • Auditory Decoys: Brass Pocket Watch [1.20], Heavy Chess Piece [3.15], Rusty Iron Hinges [7.19]
    Draws sentry attention away from the party's Hide in Shadows attempt.
  • Blinding Powders: Sack of Gravel [5.15], Plaster Dust [7.14], Coal Scuttle [7.20], Beeswax Votive Candles [10.03]
    Target is Blinded; loses AC dexterity bonuses and line-of-sight.
  • Respiratory Disruption: Heavy Pepper Mill [2.18], Ammonia Spirits [4.06], Ground Cayenne Pepper [6.17]
    Interrupts somatic/verbal components; Save vs. Poison to act.

III. The Art of Uninvited Entry

When the locksmith is unavailable and the heavy oak doors of the dungeon refuse to yield to common courtesy.

  • Improvised Lockpicks: Bone Comb [1.06], Collar Stiffeners [1.19], Waxed Playing Cards [3.13], Metal Hoof Pick [9.01]
    Allows Open Locks checks without proper Thieves' Tools.
  • Forcing the Portal: Fireplace Poker [3.09], Brass Shoehorn [6.11], Sledged Iron Mallet [7.11]
    Grants bonuses to Bend Bars/Lift Gates or Open Doors rolls.
  • Total Structural Demolition: Sledgehammer [7.18], Vinegar Cask [7.17]
    Automatically breaches standard wooden doors given time.
  • Safe Peeping: Corkscrew [2.06]
    Bores silent peepholes to view the next room before opening.

IV. Health, Hygiene & Hazard Removal

To preserve the Master's health against the cowardly mechanisms and toxic atmospheres favoured by subterranean architects.

  • Remote Trap Triggering: Hickory Walking Cane [1.08], Long-handled Snuffer [1.10], Needle & Silk Thread [1.15], Baker's Twine [6.12], Stout Hiking Staff [9.14]
    Triggers mechanisms from safely outside the blast radius.
  • Disarming & Jamming Mechanisms: Grooming Shears [1.13], Carving Fork [2.19], Brass Tweezers [4.16], Brass Cask Spigot [7.07]
    Grants +5% to Find/Remove Traps or permanently seizes gears.
  • Anti-Toxin & Filtration: Silk Handkerchiefs [1.05], Lye-Soaked Rags [7.06], Charcoal Briquettes [5.17]
    Grants bonuses to Saves vs. Inhaled/Ingested Poisons.
  • Safe Illumination: Iron Gall Ink [3.04], Mortar & Pestle (with glowing fungi) [4.14]
    Creates directional or heatless light that does not attract infravision predators.

V. Ensuring a Decorous Retreat

Should a tactical withdrawal become necessary, these implements ensure the enemy cannot follow without suffering terrible indignity.

  • Improvised Caltrops & Foot-Traps: Brass Cloak Pins [1.09], Lead Floral Frogs [6.07], Empty Vintage Bottle [7.08], Iron Rat Trap [7.16]
    Halts movement; forces Saves vs. Paralyzation.
  • Frictionless Surfaces: Brass Polish [2.11], Castor Oil [4.15], Flask of Lantern Oil [9.12]
    Creates a Grease spell effect on floors or stairs.
  • Invisible Tripwires: Heavy Jute Twine [5.05], Strong Fishing Gut [6.06], Weaver's Linen Thread [8.19]
    Bypasses magical detection; forces Dexterity checks to avoid falling prone.
  • Jamming Doors Shut: Wooden Door Wedges [2.12], Dried Lentils [6.18], Ironmonger's Nails [6.20], Iron Cask Hoops [8.01], Canvas Tent Pegs [9.15]
    Permanently spikes doors, guaranteeing safe long rests.

VI. Etiquette & Counter-Espionage

For neutralising threats whilst maintaining a flawless posture at the Baron's masquerade or the High Cleric's sermon.

  • Bypassing Weapon Confiscation: Silver Hatpin [1.02], Straight Razor [1.12], Silver Letter Opener [3.07], Cobbler's Awl [8.12], Crystal Champagne Flute [10.17]
    Improvised daggers that perfectly bypass city guard pat-downs.
  • Anti-Divination & Espionage: Papier-mache Mask [10.09], Waxed Butcher's Paper [6.15], Wet Potter's Clay [8.09]
    Blocks ESP, Hold Person line-of-sight, and keyhole snooping.
  • Discreet Poison Vectors: Brass Thimbles [8.08], Haberdasher's Pins [6.13], Sharp Calling Cards [10.13]
    Safely delivers Type M Contact or Type O Injected toxins.

VII. Pest Control

Because standard steel is frequently useless against the bizarre ecologies of Oerth's deeper basements.

  • Anti-Slime & Ooze: Lemon Juice / Vinegar [2.04], Baking Soda / Lye [2.09], Azure Sea Salts [4.05], Cured Block of Salt [7.10]
    Neutralises acidic enemies or inflicts severe dehydration damage.
  • Anti-Gaze: Woollen Cloak [1.11], Small Metal Mirror [1.16], Silver Serving Tray [3.02]
    Negates the need to Save vs. Petrification against medusae/basilisks.
  • Anti-Invisible Stalker: Sack of Fine Flour [2.05], Pounce Powder [3.08], Starch Powder [4.08], Dried Rice / Confetti [10.11]
    Coats the invisible entity, completely ruining its concealment bonuses.
  • Anti-Undead & Lycanthrope: Scarecrow Stakes [5.13], Silver Letter Opener [3.07], Flask of Holy Water [10.05], Garlic Bulbs [6.10]
    Bypasses weapon immunities or inflicts direct holy damage.
